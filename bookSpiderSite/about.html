    Using BookSpider

    Using BookSpider is as simple as performing a google search. Simply navigate to our search page and type in the name of the book you are looking for. You can also search for an author, IBSN, specific editions or years published, etc. After our spiders have crawled the web in search of your book they will return links to websites in which you can download your book. The results are generated in a table showing you the website name, book title, author, IBSN, and link to the page containing the download link.

    If you have suggestions for websites in which you have had success obtaining free versions of books before and would like us to include said website in our program, please send us a message!



    About BookSpider

    BookSpider was developed by four Computer Science students at Louisiana State University for their Object Oriented Design class in the fall of 2017. The developers conducted research to justify the demand for a service such as BookSpider. The results of their research showed that majority of students surveyed would utilize a service which scoured the internet for downloadable versions of their required textbooks. BookSpider works by taking input from users and running multiple custom web crawlers programmed by the developers. If a web crawler is able to find a download link for the input, this link is returned in a tabular format along with other data such as the website name, book title, etc.

    For any questions or concerns regarding the legality of BookSpider please refer to our disclaimer below, or feel free to contact us.
